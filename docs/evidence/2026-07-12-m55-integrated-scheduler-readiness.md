## Transactional Rapid-Input Evidence

Rapid input is resolved by explicit ownership phases, not by seeking or
restoring old pixels after a decoder reset:

- A normal route before commit retains the source path and its reserved next frame.
  Cancellation or replacement resumes at the exact displayed source frame + 1.
- A cut or reversible endpoint first stages persistent handles plus an
  identity-stable scheduler transaction. Staging does not replace the active
  decoder generation, ring, or source cursor.
- Cancelling or replacing an uncommitted resident route rolls back only that
  staged transaction. The still-visible source scheduler remains authoritative.
- The resident target's body frame zero crosses the synchronous draw barrier
  before the scheduler transaction commits. Only then does the media lane
  activate the reserved generation and start streaming continuation.
- After one or more resident runway frames are visible, that target is the
  visual authority. A later cut may supersede on the next tick; a non-cut
  follow-on remains unready until the exact continuation can preserve its
  authored start and wait bound.
- An inverse intent while a reversible endpoint is staged cancels the endpoint
  transaction and draws the adjacent reverse frame on the next tick.
- A queued third-state follow-on still draws endpoint body frame zero exactly
  once. A cut follow-on stages its own resident frame zero for the following
  tick; it does not wait for an obsolete endpoint runway to drain.
- Requests during a locked bridge preserve the complete bridge and target
  frame zero, then apply only the newest valid follow-on.
- Finite/held bodies saturate at their authored final frame. A six-tick runway
  over a four-frame finite body hands streaming ownership to terminal frame
  three and holds it; it never wraps.
- Aborting a caller-owned pump stops that operation without terminalizing the
  scheduler or stealing ownership from a newer route.

The dedicated `browser playback phase ownership` suite names these regression
cases explicitly:

- `retires an in-flight normal route without stealing its source reservation`;
- `coalesces an in-flight normal replacement to the latest edge`;
- `cancels a fully staged cut before frame zero without mutating the source path`;
- `replaces an uncommitted cut with a non-cut route and preserves source adjacency`;
- `replaces cut A with cut B before frame zero and commits only B`;
- `keeps a non-cut follow-on route unready through the visible cut runway`;
- `restores cut A after staging and cancelling cut B before B frame zero`;
- `reverses adjacently when inverse intent supersedes a staged endpoint`;
- `draws endpoint body zero before a queued third-state non-cut follow-on`;
- `draws endpoint body zero before a queued cut follow-on`;
- `enters the inverse clip on the tick after endpoint body zero`;
- `promotes a finite target longer than the ring and reaches its terminal hold`;
- `hands a finite body4 runway6 to terminal streaming ownership`;
- `stages a completion cut at the final frame of a visible finite runway`;
- `publishes an implicit non-cut completion route at the finite terminal`;
- `lets an explicit non-cut request supersede a staged completion cut at the final frame`;
- `rejects recovery that consumes the frame-zero draw from the whole runway`;
  and
- `keeps the scheduler active when a caller aborts an in-flight pump`.

The lower-level scheduler suite separately proves staged rollback, exact
generation commit, stale-token rejection, source-reservation retention, long
finite handoff, and terminal finite cancellation. These tests are named
`rolls back a staged runway without disturbing a source reservation`, `commits
the exact staged generation and records drawn frame zero directly`, `does not
let a stale token rollback a newer staged runway`, `discards a reserved route
without advancing and replaces it in-place`, and `cancels an uncommitted route
at a terminal finite source`.

## Trace, Effects, Promises, and Reentrancy

`IntegratedTraceHarness` retains at most 512 immutable, handle-free records.
Every content record correlates presentation ordinal and rational deadline,
graph operation/snapshot/presentation/effects, `routeReady`, selected boundary,
scheduler snapshot, submitted cursors, resident/streaming media identity,
readback tag, readiness, decode lead, settlement IDs, and bounded
underflow/fallback/settled/cleanup counters. Scheduler traces separately record
activate, submit, output, discard/stale output, present, resident present,
route select/commit, generation retirement, underflow, watchdog, failure, and
disposal with exact identity fields.

The browser proof enforces this animated order:

```text
request
< transitionstart
< first reversible draw
< target body frame-zero draw
< visualstatechange
< transitionend
< request-promise settlement
```

The induced fatal-worker recovery enforces:

```text
recovery request
< fallback event
< requested static draw
< visualstatechange
< transitionend
< surviving request-promise settlement
```

At the target draw, public `visualState` is still the source and
`isTransitioning` is true. At the later visual event and promise settlement,
the staged fields expose the committed target. Stable no-ops and graph settle
effects resolve in a following microtask; superseded requests reject with
`AbortError`.

Listener-originated requests are FIFO-deferred until the outer activation,
effect, draw, and playback synchronization transaction returns. Nested manual
ticks and realtime starts are rejected rather than interleaved; reentrant
disposal is deferred and remains idempotent. Named regression tests cover
interactive-ready, visual-state, and transition-start listeners plus the exact
nested tick, while `IntegratedOperationGate` proves FIFO drain order and error
settlement independently.

Four fixed fuzz seeds (`0x1`, `0x5eedc0de`, `0xc0ffee`, and `0xffffffff`) replay
the bounded integrated model twice and require byte-for-byte identical reports.

## Claim Boundary

M5.5 demonstrates that one completely resident, validated, opaque asset can
prepare and present every declared direct route in
the recorded supported Chromium environment without seeking, decoder
reconfiguration, reset, flush, boundary flush, or an invented transition. It
may claim exact authored route order, resident next-tick cut and active inverse
response, measured upload-inclusive readiness, bounded cadence in the test
harness, staged effect/promise ordering, and recoverable animation failure to
the newest accepted static state.

It may not claim physical display scan-out continuity. Headless RAF ordering,
GPU readback, and framebuffer tags prove the runtime's submission and
presentation contract only.

M5.5 also does not claim:

- packed alpha, alpha-aware geometry, premultiplied compositing, independent
  PNG chunk/CRC/inflate/filter validation, reduced-motion policy, or background
  correctness (M6);
- network range loading, runtime digest authentication, external integrity,
  shared page budgets, eviction, visibility suspension, source replacement,
  or WebGL context restoration (M7);
- a public custom element, automatic pointer/focus/engagement bindings, DOM
  event naming, accessibility integration, or final public naming (M8); or
- 1,000-boundary release certification across named headed browsers, hardware,
  refresh rates, power modes, and observed displays (M9).

H.264/AVC deployment and source/derived media may carry patent, codec-license,
content-license, and distribution obligations. A caller-installed encoder does
not remove them; product-specific legal review remains required before release.
